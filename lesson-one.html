Preamble

noun

An introductory statement; a preliminary explanation

Example: The preamble to the Constitution outlines the fundamental purposes of the document.

Presage

verb

To foretell or predict; to give an omen or warning of

Example: The dark clouds presage a coming storm.

Prescient

adjective

Having knowledge of events before they take place; prophetic

Example: Her prescient warning about the market crash saved many investors from losses.

Predilection

noun

A preference or special liking for something; a bias in favor of something

Example: She has a predilection for classical music over modern pop.

Precipitate

verb

To cause something to happen suddenly or too soon; to bring about abruptly

Example: The CEO's resignation could precipitate a major reorganization of the company.

Predicate

verb

To base or establish something on; to affirm or assert

Example: The success of the project is predicated on everyone's full participation.

Presentiment

noun

A feeling that something is about to happen, especially something bad; a premonition

Example: She had a strange presentiment that something was wrong before she received the phone call.
